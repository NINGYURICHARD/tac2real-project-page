Overview

This section summarizes the story of the paper in project-page form, so visitors can quickly understand the motivation, the technical contribution, and the experimental takeaways before reading the full manuscript.

Problem

Contact-rich robot learning with visuotactile sensors is hard to transfer from simulation to reality because contact deformation, marker motion, and sensor response are difficult to model accurately.

Core Idea

Tac2Real builds a sim-to-real pipeline that better captures tactile deformation behavior and uses it to support training and evaluation for manipulation policies.

Outcome

The paper compares Tac2Real against prior tactile simulation baselines and shows stronger alignment with real tactile behavior and better task-level learning performance.

Highlights

Technical Contributions

  • A marker-based visuotactile sim-to-real pipeline centered on Tac2Real.
  • Explicit modeling of tactile deformation and contact response.
  • Evaluation across simulation realism, efficiency, and downstream manipulation.

Experiments

  • Comparison to other tactile simulation methods such as Tacchi and TacSL.
  • Qualitative and quantitative analysis over multiple contact shapes and actions.
  • Policy training results on representative tasks including peg and nut interaction.
